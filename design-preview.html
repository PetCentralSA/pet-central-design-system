01

Visual theme & atmosphere

The feeling the interface creates before anyone reads a word.

Atmosphere

Pet Central feels like a calm, modern veterinary practice in Cape Town. Warm but unmistakably clinical, the sort of place you trust with your dog. Not playful pet-store kitsch, not sterile hospital white. Think the visual register of a modern wellness brand crossed with the practical clarity of a health-tracking app.

Reassuring Considered Bright but not loud Generous with whitespace Quietly competent

What it is not

Cartoonish Pawprint-laden Pastel baby colours Heavy gradients Glassmorphism AI-generic defaults

Designed for

28 to 55 year old urban South African with disposable income who treats their pet as family. Uses Discovery Health, shops at Woolworths, listens to podcasts, and would be embarrassed by anything twee.

Clear space

Minimum clear space around the mark equals the cap height of the 'P' (marked as x below). Nothing sits inside that boundary — not text, not other logos, not UI chrome.

Minimum size

Never render the wordmark below 16px in height. Below that, the letterforms collapse and legibility fails. Use the isolated "PC" monogram for smaller contexts (favicon, app icon).

Rules

When & how
Navy, not black. Never use pure black as the page background. The brand's dark surface is `brand.primary-deep` (#163E6F) deepened to #0D2A4D.
Cream text, not white. Use `brand.cream` (#FFFCFB) for body text, not pure white. Pure white vibrates against saturated navy.
Elevation via tint, not shadow. Elevated surfaces shift lighter, not shadow-heavier. Card = #163E6F, elevated = #1E4C7F.
Borders are cream at 14%. Dark mode borders use `rgba(255, 252, 251, 0.14)` — subtle enough to recede, bright enough to read.
Primary buttons invert. On dark, primary becomes `brand.cream` background with `brand.primary-deep` text. Makes the CTA pop without shouting.
Terracotta is the accent. Stat highlights, badges, attention moments — `brand.terracotta` (#F87644) reads warmly against navy and draws the eye.

Rules

✓
One CTA per nav. Either "Book a call" or "Sign in" — never both. Compete and they cancel.
✓
Nav links read as nouns. "Brands", "Pricing", "About". Not "Discover our brands" or "See pricing".
✕
Don't deeplink the logo elsewhere. The logo always returns to the home page. No exceptions.
✕
Don't stack three columns of footer links. Two link columns max. More than that and the footer becomes a sitemap.

11

Voice & copy

Calm, warm, plainspoken. Like a vet nurse who's been doing this for fifteen years.

Tone

Do
  • South African English — "behaviour," "favourite," currency as R489
  • Pet name first — "Bella's vaccination is due"
  • Action-oriented — "Reorder Bravecto"
  • Specific — "Tick treatment due tomorrow"
  • Sentence case everywhere — headings, buttons, labels
Never
  • "Fur baby," "pawrent," "doggo," "pupper"
  • Exclamation marks (except genuinely celebratory, max one per screen)
  • Emoji in body copy
  • Second-person plural ("we") — speak as a calm individual
  • Fear-based copy — "Don't let your pet get sick!"
  • Mid-sentence bolding

Navy-tinted shadows, not black. Keeps elevated surfaces inside the brand. Use sparingly — the system is flat by default.
